Vision

We envision a world where girls in East Africa live healthy, safe, educated lives while defining their own purpose-a world in which menstrual health management is recognized as a human right, and the onset of puberty as the most effective time to engage girls in a range of personal health decisions.

Mission

Nakuhitaji equips adolescent girls in Kenya with the tools they need to safely navigate puberty and step into their potential, while also leading global advocacy efforts to break the period taboo. We are a pioneering voice in menstrual health management that leverages reproductive health education and sanitary pads as a combined intervention for women and girls’ empowerment.

The Issue

In Kenya, as in many countries, adolescent girls are at risk of experiencing life-changing violations, including forced early marriage, sexual and gender-based violence, unplanned pregnancy, and female genital cutting.

Such pressures are insurmountable without knowledge of their bodies and rights; yet, there is no mandated menstrual and reproductive health education (MRHE) to deliver this critical help. If girls cannot get answers to their questions about puberty, or the tools for decision-making, they cannot make informed decisions in social or sexual relationships. Compounding this challenge, 65% of girls in Kenya cannot regularly access sanitary pads to manage their periods.

Founder's Story

Emma Moffat is an Professional Corporate Communicator for the CAK, Women and Girls' Rights Advocate and Founder of Nakuhitaji Organization. She is an inspiration to millions of young women in africa and across the globe for her effort towards period poverty advocacy.

Nakuhitaji was founded in 2017 and is based in Nairobi. It's offices are distributed across africa and few countries abroad. She is also the Director of Moftech Digital solutions and Co-founder of MoFEMA Talent School that offers street and orphaned children an opportunity to learn and discover their talents.
